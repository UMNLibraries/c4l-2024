University of Minnesota Code4Lib 2024

Why Minneapolis

The University of Minnesota Twin Cities is one of the nation's top public research universities, with an award-winning library system to match. The University of Minnesota Libraries are excited to invite the Code4Lib community back to the midwest, to our hometown, and to our beloved campus. We propose to host this event in the very heart of our school: Coffman Memorial Union. Come visit the institution that invented the pacemaker, the retractable seat belt, and the Honey Crisp apple. Come meet library colleagues who launched the Data Curation Network, lead the Mapping Prejudice Project, and podcast the History of Transgender Activist Communities. Wherever your professional interests lie, you'll find your warm and dedicated counterparts here at the University of Minnesota.

The city of Minneapolis and the state of Minnesota are a welcoming place for everyone. Minneapolis is a vibrant and diverse town known for its parks, lakes, art museums, theater, food, and music venues. The Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport (MSP) has been named the "Best Airport in North America" for the second straight year, and the city has great public transportation options. Ride our light rail from the airport directly to your hotel downtown, and immediately curbside to the conference venue on campus.

Venue

Code4Lib would be hosted on campus, in the Minneapolis Campus’ Great Hall in Coffman Memorial Union (CMU). CMU has numerous dining options.

  • Preconference workshops: Coffman Memorial Union's 3rd floor
  • Main conference: Coffman Memorial Union's Great Hall
  • Reception: Weismann Art Museum's Riverview Gallery or Shepherd Room

The venue can host up to 200 attendees.

Cost

For attendees:

  • Registration fee: about $200
  • Hotels: between $100 and $300

Hotels

There are several large hotels within walking distance and convenient to transit.

  • The Graduate (136 rooms, $211/night), 0.4 miles from the venue and directly adjacent to the venue's light rail station
  • Courtyard by Marriott Minneapolis Downtown (265 rooms, $190/night), 0.7 miles from the venue and adjacent to a light rail station, one stop to venue or 15 minute walk
  • Hilton Garden Inn Minneapolis University Area (140 rooms, $185/night), 0.8 miles from the venue and 20 minute walk
  • Days Hotel University ($135/night), 0.7 miles from the venu and adjacent to a light rail station, one stop to venue or 15 minute walk

Many more hotels are located in downtown Minneapolis, just 16 minutes from the venue via the light rail. Rail service runs every 15 minutes.

Travel

Getting here

Minneapolis is well connected via airlines, trains, and driving.

  • Minneapolis-St.Paul International airport (MSP) is a hub for Delta airlines. MSP has 756 arrivals per day, so it’s easy to get to from almost anywhere in the US. The airport is only 18 minutes by car or 41 minutes by light rail (same station transfer required) from the venue.
  • Amtrak’s Empire Builder has daily service from Chicago, Portland, Oregon, and Seattle through St. Paul’s Union Depot. Summer 2023 will see the launch of the Great River train from Chicago to St. Paul via Milwaukee, with a second daily round trip potentially starting in Autumn 2023. Union Depot is 30 minutes direct via light rail or 20 minutes by car.
  • Minneapolis is served by Interstates 35 and 94, with Interstate 90 approximately 90 minutes south.

Getting around

The University’s Minneapolis campus is very well served by Metro Transit and campus buses. While large, the conference venue is centrally located and less than 7 minutes’ walk (slightly less than 0.3 miles) from the Graduate Hotel.

Extras

The 3 campuses that comprise the University of Minnesota Twin Cities, as well as Minneapolis and St. Paul, have many opportunities and activities. Explore the Gopher Way (the underground walkway system to bypass the cold weather), traverse across the Mississippi River through the covered Washington Ave bridge walkway, explore Dinkytown, or join some tours.

On campus

Some possible tours we can offer around campus include:

  • Minnesota Library Access Center (MLAC) cavern tours (West Bank, high-density storage details)
  • Health Sciences Library building tour including VR studio (East Bank)
  • Raptor Center tour (St. Paul campus)
  • Conservatory and Botanical Collection tour (St. Paul campus)
  • Weisman Art Museum (East Bank)
  • Huntington Bank Stadium tour

Around town

The Twin Cities offers a full array of activities for different interests.

  • On Beckett (“a one-man tribute to a literary legend”) playing at the Guthrie Theater (1.3 miles, 25 minute walk or 10-15 minute bus ride on the 3, 465, or 475)
  • First Avenue live music (2.3 miles, about 20 minutes on the Green line)
  • Women's Big10 basketball tournament (2.5 miles to Target Center, 20 minutes using the Green line)
  • Minneapolis Institute of Art (free, 2.6 miles, about 30 minutes on the 2 bus)
  • Walker Art Center and sculpture garden (3.3 miles, 35 minute ride on the 2 bus)
  • Mall of America (12 miles, hop the lightrail to get there in an hour - requires a transfer from the Green line downtown to the Blue line)
